You can chat with AI when you want speed, or&#xa0;edit visually when you want to update or refine your dashboard.</p><p>This guide walks you through building dashboards on Bubble, the only fully visual AI app builder. You&#x2019;ll learn how to create dashboards that drive decisions and deploy with one click across web and native mobile apps.</p><h2 id="define-your-dashboard%E2%80%99s-goals-and-key-metrics">Define your dashboard&#x2019;s goals and key metrics</h2><p>A dashboard is a visual display of your most important data, designed to help you make specific decisions faster. This means that before you touch any tools, you need to define exactly what decisions your dashboard should accelerate. Without this clarity, you&#x2019;ll end up with a cluttered screen full of metrics that look impressive but don&#x2019;t actually help anyone.</p><p>Start by identifying your primary users and their needs. An executive dashboard tracking company revenue needs different metrics than a manager&#x2019;s dashboard showing daily team productivity. Each audience makes different decisions and needs different information at different levels of detail.</p><p>Focus on actionable metrics that drive specific decisions, not vanity metrics that just look good. For example, total website visitors is a vanity metric, but <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/web-analytics/">conversion rate by traffic source</a> is actionable because you can decide where to invest your marketing budget. Ask yourself: &#x201c;If this number changes, what would I do differently?&#x201d; If you can&#x2019;t answer that question, the metric probably doesn&#x2019;t belong on your dashboard.</p><p>Limit yourself to five to nine key metrics maximum. More info may seem more helpful, but research confirms that users become <a href="https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0926580523002893">overwhelmed beyond nine information modules</a>. Connect each metric to a specific action or decision point, and define what success looks like for your specific use case, whether that&#x2019;s faster response times, better forecast accuracy, or clearer visibility into team performance.</p><div class="kg-card kg-callout-card kg-callout-card-blue"><div class="kg-callout-emoji">&#x1f4a1;</div><div class="kg-callout-text"><b><strong style="white-space: pre-wrap;">Avoid dashboard bloat:</strong></b> Resist the urge to show everything you can measure. The best dashboards show only what matters most for the decisions you need to make today. You can always create additional dashboards for different use cases or audiences.</div></div><h2 id="connect-and-prepare-your-data-sources">Connect and prepare your data sources</h2><p>Data connections form the foundation of any dashboard. Every dashboard needs data from somewhere, whether that&#x2019;s a database storing customer records, spreadsheets tracking sales, or APIs pulling real-time metrics from external services. The quality and structure of these connections directly impact your dashboard&#x2019;s usefulness and performance.</p><p>Bubble includes a built-in database and lets you connect external sources like APIs and spreadsheets&#xa0;through a visual interface. The <a href="https://bubble.io/ai-agent">Bubble AI Agent</a> (beta) can help you structure your data: Ask it to create data types and fields, and it will automatically add privacy rules for sensitive information.</p><h3 id="set-up-data-connections-and-relationships">Set up data connections and relationships</h3><p>Start by listing all the data sources you need. Most dashboards pull from multiple sources: internal databases, third-party services, spreadsheets, and APIs. Understanding what data lives where helps you plan your connections strategically.</p><p>Bubble includes a built-in database for your core data, and the <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/api-connector/">API Connector</a> (now in its own editor tab) lets you connect to any REST API &#x2014; including Salesforce, HubSpot, and Google Sheets &#x2014; while the SQL Database Connector plugin lets you connect to PostgreSQL, MySQL, and Microsoft SQL.</p><p>Once connected, you need to define <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/design-relational-database/">relationships between different datasets</a>.&#xa0;Data relationships use common fields to link different <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/what-is-database/">tables or datasets</a> together. Think of it like a digital filing system where customer IDs, timestamps, or product codes act as connecting threads between different pieces of information. Setting these up correctly ensures your dashboard can answer complex questions across your entire data landscape.</p><h3 id="clean-and-structure-data-for-dashboard-use">Clean and structure data for dashboard use</h3><p>Clean data means accurate, consistent information without duplicates or missing values. Before any dashboard can deliver insights, the underlying data needs preparation. Remove duplicate entries, handle empty cells appropriately, and standardize formats across your datasets. For example, make sure all dates use the same format and all currency values include the same decimal places.</p><p>Organize your data in dashboard-friendly formats with clear, descriptive column headers. Avoid technical database naming conventions that make sense to developers but confuse business users. Instead of &#x201c;cust_acq_dt,&#x201d; use &#x201c;Customer Acquisition Date.&#x201d; In Bubble, this clarity helps when you&#x2019;re selecting data fields for charts and filters later: The AI Agent and visual editor both display these field names, so descriptive labels make building faster and more intuitive.</p><p>Create realistic sample data to validate your dashboard logic before connecting to live production data. Sample data lets you test whether calculations work correctly, charts display properly, and filters behave as expected. This testing phase catches errors early when they&#x2019;re easier to fix.</p><h3 id="configure-privacy-rules-and-access-controls">Configure privacy rules and access controls</h3><p><a href="https://bubble.io/blog/customer-data-privacy/">Customer data privacy</a> isn&#x2019;t an afterthought &#x2014; it&#x2019;s essential from the start. Every dashboard handling user information needs security controls that determine who can access which data. Row-level security filters data based on user attributes or team membership. A regional manager logging into a sales dashboard would see only their region&#x2019;s data, while a VP sees all regions. Field-level permissions control access to sensitive columns like salary information, personal contact details, or confidential business metrics.</p><p>Bubble&#x2019;s <a href="https://bubble.io/security">visual privacy rules</a> let you control who can see which data without code. When the AI Agent creates data types, it automatically generates privacy rules for sensitive information. For example, you can configure sales reps to see only their own customer data, managers to see their team&#x2019;s data, and executives to see company-wide information.&#xa0;This security happens at the data layer, ensuring sensitive information never reaches unauthorized users. Even if someone can access a dataset, you can hide specific fields that contain sensitive information they shouldn&#x2019;t see.</p><p><strong>Important:</strong> Bubble&#x2019;s Data Processing Addendum prohibits processing certain sensitive data types including Social Security numbers, biometric information, passwords, financial account credentials, payment card information subject to PCI DSS, and personal data of children under 16. Review the DPA before processing personal data to ensure compliance.</p><h2 id="generate-your-dashboard-foundation-with-ai">Generate your dashboard foundation with AI</h2><p>With data connected, Bubble AI can generate a complete dashboard foundation in minutes &#x2014; all visual, never code. It creates appropriate charts, layouts, and sample calculations that you can see and understand in <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/visual-programming/">visual workflows</a>. The AI Agent helps you refine your dashboard through natural language prompts or you can edit directly in the visual editor.</p><h3 id="craft-effective-prompts-for-dashboard-generation">Craft effective prompts for dashboard generation</h3><p><a href="https://bubble.io/blog/asking-bubble-ai-right-questions/">Structure your prompts</a> to include four key elements: audience, metrics, time periods, and interaction requirements. For example, you can ask the Agent to &#x201c;add a comparison to last year&#x201d; or edit the chart type yourself with a few clicks.</p><p>The more context you provide about how the dashboard will be used, the better Bubble AI can optimize the layout and interactions. Tell it whether users will view your dashboard on large screens in conference rooms, individual desktop monitors, or mobile devices (or all of the above). These details influence everything from chart sizing to filter placement.</p><h3 id="review-and-validate-your-ai-generated-components">Review and validate your AI-generated components</h3><p>After Bubble AI generates your dashboard, you can see exactly what it created in visual workflows, not hidden code. Validate the following:</p><ul><li><strong>Chart types match your data:</strong> Bar charts for comparisons, line charts for trends, pie charts for part-to-whole relationships. Ask the AI Agent to explain any component, and it will tell you what it built and why.</li><li><strong>Data fields connect correctly:</strong> Verify that Bubble AI connected the right data fields to charts. If it used &#x201c;Order Date&#x201d; when you wanted &#x201c;Ship Date,&#x201d; simply ask the AI Agent to fix it &#x2014; or change the data source yourself in the visual editor.</li><li><strong>Calculations produce expected results:</strong> Test with known data points. If you know last month&#x2019;s total revenue was $50,000, verify that the dashboard shows that exact number. Run through different filter combinations to ensure the dashboard updates correctly and maintains accuracy across all scenarios.</li></ul><h2 id="customize-visuals-and-add-interactivity">Customize visuals and add interactivity</h2><p>Once you have your dashboard foundation in place, the next step is customization &#x2014; refining visuals and adding interactive elements that transform static charts into dynamic tools users can actually explore. This is where your dashboard evolves from displaying data to enabling discovery.</p><p>Bubble&#xa0;gives you complete flexibility to customize your dashboard. Chat with the AI Agent for quick adjustments, or jump into the visual editor when you want precise control&#xa0;over every detail. Add interactive elements like filters, drill-downs, and dynamic controls so users can slice data from different angles&#xa0;and find the insights most relevant to their needs.</p><h3 id="choose-the-right-visualization-types-for-your-data">Choose the right visualization types for your data</h3><p>Picking the right chart type makes your dashboard instantly more useful. Here&#x2019;s what works best for different scenarios:</p><ul><li><strong>Bar charts</strong> are great for comparing values across categories &#x2014; think revenue by product line or performance by team member.</li><li><strong>Line charts</strong> reveal trends and patterns over time, making them perfect for tracking metrics like daily active users or monthly sales growth.</li><li><strong>Tables</strong> work best when users need exact values or detailed breakdowns that visualizations would hide. Use them sparingly though &#x2014; they take more mental effort to interpret than charts.</li><li><strong>Gauge charts</strong> effectively display single metrics against targets, like showing quarterly revenue at 87% of goal.</li></ul><p>Once you&#x2019;ve chosen your chart types, use size, color, and position to guide attention to what matters most. Place critical metrics in the top-left corner where eyes naturally start scanning. Make primary charts larger than secondary ones. And use color strategically to highlight performance: green for on-track, yellow for caution, red for concerning.</p><h3 id="add-filters-drill-downs-and-interactive-elements">Add filters, drill-downs, and interactive elements</h3><p>Filters let users customize their view without creating multiple dashboard versions. Here are the most useful types:</p><ul><li><strong>Date range filters</strong> are nearly universal. Most dashboards benefit from letting users select specific time periods to analyze.</li><li><strong>Dropdown selectors</strong> work well for categorical filters like region, product category, or customer segment.</li><li><strong>Multi-select options</strong> give users flexibility to compare specific subsets of data. For example, a marketing dashboard might let users select multiple campaign types to compare their performance side by side.</li><li><strong>Search boxes</strong> help when you have many filter options &#x2014; think searching through hundreds of customer names or thousands of product SKUs.</li><li><strong>Drill-down navigation</strong> enables users to click from summary views into detailed breakdowns. Clicking on a revenue chart showing all regions might navigate to a detailed view of that specific region&#x2019;s performance. This progressive disclosure keeps the main dashboard uncluttered while still providing access to deeper analysis when needed.</li><li><strong>Hover tooltips</strong> display exact values and additional context without cluttering the visual.</li><li><strong>Dynamic parameter controls</strong> let advanced users adjust calculation methods or comparison periods on the fly. Keep these advanced features accessible but not prominent. Most users want the default view to just work.</li></ul><h2 id="design-for-clarity-and-performance">Design for clarity and performance</h2><p>A gorgeous dashboard that takes forever to load or leaves users scratching their heads? That&#x2019;s a failed dashboard. Simple design principles can turn a jumble of charts into a story that actually makes sense. And performance optimization keeps everything snappy, whether you&#x2019;re dealing with massive datasets or hundreds of users hitting your dashboard at once.</p><p>Style variables are your friend here. Define your colors, fonts, and spacing once&#xa0;in your app&#x2019;s style variables, and they&#x2019;ll apply consistently across your entire dashboard. The AI Agent automatically picks up these style variables when it generates new elements, so everything looks cohesive and professional without extra work.</p><p>When it comes to organizing your metrics, think in logical groups using containers:</p><ul><li>Put all your revenue metrics together in one section</li><li>Give customer metrics their own dedicated space</li><li>Keep operational metrics separate from financial data</li></ul><p>This kind of grouping isn&#x2019;t just about looking organized &#x2014; it genuinely helps users find what they need faster and spot connections between related&#xa0;metrics.</p><p>On the performance side,&#xa0;Bubble&#x2019;s built-in database and auto-scaling infrastructure do the heavy lifting for you. <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/improve-web-app-performance/">Performance optimization</a> happens automatically. If you&#x2019;re working with large datasets, try smart data loading. For example, you can show just the current month when the dashboard first loads, then let users expand the date range&#xa0;if they want to dig deeper. Bubble&#x2019;s infrastructure handles everything from 10 users to 10,000+ seamlessly.</p><p>Don&#x2019;t forget to test your dashboard on different devices. Bubble AI generates responsive designs by default, but you&#x2019;ll want to fine-tune how your charts and tables look on mobile to make sure the experience is smooth everywhere.</p><div class="kg-card kg-callout-card kg-callout-card-blue"><div class="kg-callout-emoji">&#x1f3af;</div><div class="kg-callout-text"><b><strong style="white-space: pre-wrap;">The five-second rule:</strong></b> If someone can&#x2019;t grasp your dashboard&#x2019;s main message within five seconds of looking at it, something needs to change. Either simplify what you&#x2019;re showing or improve your visual hierarchy so the most important stuff jumps out immediately.</div></div><h2 id="publish-and-share-your-dashboard-securely">Publish and share your dashboard securely</h2><p>Once your dashboard is ready, the final step is making it available to users, whether that&#x2019;s your internal team, external clients, or mobile app users. Decide whether your dashboard requires authentication, what access different user types need, and how to track data viewing. These decisions shape everything from login requirements to audit trails.</p><p>Bubble&#x2019;s <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/sso-integration/">SSO integration</a> (available on Enterprise plans) lets users access dashboards with existing company credentials. Logs capture user identity, timestamp, specific data viewed, and actions taken.</p><p>Bubble provides a <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/bubble-gdpr/">GDPR-compliant Data Processing Addendum</a>&#xa0;(DPA) across all plans for apps processing personal data. The DPA also covers major U.S. state privacy laws including CCPA/CPRA, Colorado Privacy Act, Connecticut Data Privacy Act, Utah Consumer Privacy Act, and Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act.</p><h3 id="choose-your-sharing-method">Choose your sharing method</h3><p>Different use cases require different sharing approaches. Here are the four main methods for deploying your dashboard:</p><p><strong>Internal team access</strong> works well for company dashboards and team metrics. This method offers the highest security through SSO and role-based access controls, with full control over all elements and deployment to both web and native mobile apps. It&#x2019;s particularly useful when your dashboard contains sensitive business data that only authenticated employees should see.</p><p><strong>Public sharing with authentication</strong> fits client portals and partner dashboards. This medium-security approach relies on password or token-based authentication. Users can view and interact with the dashboard, though customization is limited to viewing preferences. The dashboard works responsively on web browsers across all devices.</p><p><strong>Embedded in apps</strong> integrates dashboards into custom portals and internal tools. This high-security method uses API tokens and domain restrictions to protect your data, with programmatic control over styling, authentication, and data filtering. The result is a seamless experience where the dashboard feels native to your app, with mobile support inherited from the host application.</p><p><strong>Public anonymous access</strong> fits marketing metrics and status pages. This low-security option requires no authentication, making it perfect for publicly shareable data. Users get view-only access with minimal interaction, and the dashboard works responsively across web browsers on all devices.</p><h3 id="deploy-across-web-and-mobile-platforms">Deploy across web and mobile platforms</h3><p>Modern dashboards need to work everywhere your users are: desktop browsers, tablets, and mobile devices. Desktop users see the full layout with multiple charts side by side, while mobile users see a vertically stacked version optimized for scrolling. You can fine-tune responsive behavior with Bubble&#x2019;s visual responsive design tools, no code required.</p><p><a href="https://bubble.io/mobile">Build native iOS and Android apps</a> alongside your web dashboard from the same Bubble editor. They share the same database, workflows, and backend. Bubble&#x2019;s native mobile apps run on React Native, enabling <a href="https://bubble.io/blog/cross-platform-mobile-app-development/">cross-platform development</a> from a single codebase.</p><p>You can generate mobile apps with Bubble AI just like web apps, then submit directly to the App Store and Google Play from within Bubble. Test with BubbleGo, a testing app that lets you preview your native mobile apps on actual iOS and Android devices. (You&#x2019;ll need a paid plan for TestFlight and Google Play testing, as well as app store publishing.)</p><p>Load times are also key. Bubble&#x2019;s auto-scaling infrastructure and global CDN ensure fast loading times worldwide, automatically optimizing for different connection speeds. Test your dashboard on different devices using BubbleGo for mobile apps or the built-in responsive preview for web.</p><p>Bubble handles traffic spikes and slow connections automatically so you focus on building, not infrastructure. DDoS protection is included through Cloudflare integration, with Enterprise plans able to customize Cloudflare configuration for advanced protection.</p><h3 id="embed-dashboards-in-existing-apps">Embed dashboards in existing apps</h3><p>Embedding lets you integrate your dashboard directly into existing web apps or internal tools. iFrame embedding is the simplest method: It involves copying a code snippet and pasting it into your app&#x2019;s HTML. Thisapproach works well for basic embedding but offers limited options for customization.</p><p>API-based embedding provides more control over styling, user authentication, and data filtering. Your app can programmatically control which data the embedded dashboard displays based on the logged-in user&#x2019;s permissions and context. This creates a seamless experience where the dashboard feels like a native part of your app rather than a separate tool.</p><p>You can configure security for embedded dashboards using token-based authentication and domain restrictions. Restrict embedding to specific, trusted domains to prevent unauthorized websites from embedding your dashboard. Session-based tokens that expire after a set time help minimize security risks if tokens are compromised.</p>
